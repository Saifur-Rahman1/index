All Beautiful Place In Narayanganj

  • Sonargaon Museum

    • The Bangladesh Folk and Crafts Foundation is known as the Sonargaon Museum. Its distance from the capital Dhaka is only 24 kilometers.Sonargaon was once world famous for muslin. An alternative to muslin, Jamdani Sharee can still be seen directly inside the Karupalli. There is the Folk and Crafts Foundation Museum.sha Khan, one of the chiefs of the Bara Bhuiyans, ruled Sonargaon for a long time. Surrounded by rivers, Sonargaon could not be easily attacked by any enemy.The Bangladesh Folk and Crafts Museum was established here in 1985.

  • Kadam Rasul Darbar Sharif

    • Kadam Rasul Darbar Sharif is located in Nabiganj area just opposite of Narayanganj city. To reach the dargah, one has to climb a high hill. There is a huge archway at the entrance of the Dargah.In the 17th century, the historian Mirza Nathan first mentioned the stone of Kadam Rasool Dargah in his famous book Bahir-Sthanai Ghayebi. Masum Khan Kabuli, the Afghan army chief who declared rebellion against Emperor Akbar in 1580, bought the stone from an Arab merchant with the footprints of the Prophet Muhammad (Peace be upon him). Much later, Golam Nabi, the Zamindar of Dhaka, built a single-domed dargah on a high place in Nabiganj in 1777-1778 and placed the sacred stone there. From then on, his name was Rasool. Later, in 1814, Ghulam Muhammad, son of Ghulam Nabi, built the main gate of the Dargah.

  • Panam Nagar

    • Panam Nagar, known as the lost city an archaeological site in Bangladesh situated at Sonargaon Upazila of Narayanganj District You will be astonished to know that the structures are older than around years. This is called the oldest capital of Bengal and the ancient silk road from the Bengal delta to China and Central Asia.As the capital of the fifteenth-century Bengal ruler Isa Khan, and once an important trading and political center, Sonargaon boasts architecture of the Sultanate, Mughal, and colonial periods. The Sonargaon historic city, located near the present-day capital of Dhaka, includes several Mughal monumentsamong them the Sonakanda River Fort, the Panch Pir Mazar Shrine, and Ibrahim's and Abdul Hamid's Mosque. British colonial architecture preserved in Sonargaon includes the Ananda Mohan Piddar House, and other street-front houses. For travelers making their way along the 2,500-kilometer Grand Trunk Road from Peshawar in the Hindu Kush, Sonargaon marked the end of the line. Threats to the site include flooding, vandalism, unauthorized occupation, illegal development, etc.

  • Sabdi Narayanganj (Flower Village)

    • Sabdi in Narayanganj district is an interesting place to visit from Dhaka. In winter, the crop fields of Sabdi village are full of yellow mustard flowers. Besides, different types of flowers like rose, marigold, gladiolus, tuberose, gerbera, cosmos, daisy gypsy, dahlia, chrysanthemum etc. are cultivated here in February.

  • Kaikertek Hat,Narayanganj

    • For hundreds of years, Kaikertek Hat has been held every Sunday in the Kaikertek area of Mograpara union in Sonargaon upazila of Narayanganj district. This hat, which cherishes the tradition of rural Bengal, is also known as Rabibar Hat to the locals. The river Brahmaputra flows past Kaikartek Hat. The hundred-year-old monuments and the natural environment on the banks of the river attract a variety of travelers alike, in addition to the buyers and sellers who come to Kaikartek Bazar. People from far and wide including Narsingdi, Munshiganj and Comilla flock to this Kaikartek market.

  • The Taj Mahal of Bengal

    • The Mughal emperor Shah Jahan became immortal for love. They built the Taj Mahal for Bibi. How many people in the world do not want to go there? But who can go? The good news for those who are thirsty for travel is that the second Taj Mahal in the world has been built in our city. Although not the original Taj Mahal built by Emperor Shah Jahan, this Taj Mahal has been built in the style of the Taj Mahal in Agra. Freedom fighter Ahsan Ullah Mani has built the Taj Mahal of Bengal in the Perabo area of Sonargaon in Narayanganj. To see this Taj Mahal you have to go to Perabo village in Sonargaon. The entrance fee is 50 Taka.
